May 24th, 2012 by Michael Rose
Carmel Cube Day

The Carmel Cube Day cubing competition took place on Wednesday, May 23rd, 2012, at Our Lady of Mount Carmel Secondary School. A total of 20 cubers competed in five different events. The day was a blast and results are now posted. Congratulations to all participants and thank you for your enthusiasm!

Quite a lot of preparation went in to this day, so there are many thank yous to be made. First of all, thank you to Chloe Rose and Halina Rose for the great work they did preparing and setting up the amazing decorations for the foyer, as well as the complimentary snacks that everyone enjoyed. No expression of gratitude is enough for what you both did for the day!

Next, a thank you to those who helped with the execution of the cubing, including: Matthew Murray, Bryan Melchior, Nicholas Murray, Jason Perry, and Shawn Jozsa. Thank you all for judging, scrambling, and sharing your cubes, the day would have been ten times more overwhelming without your help!

As for the results of the competition, Michael Rose won the 3x3 event with an average of 16.46 seconds. Matthew Murray placed second with an average of 33.44, and Ashley Antony placed third with an average of 37.81. Once again, congratulations to all participants, the full results from all events are now posted.

A final thank you to everyone who was a part of the day in any way. As I'm sure we will all attest, it was quite an impressive show, evident from the masses of non-cubers gawking at our solves. I cannot say how proud I am of everyone who has been a part of cubing club this year. Thank you all for your support, and I would say that we managed to put on a fantastic final time trials! Hopefully, pictures from the day will soon be posted on the site, Facebook, or both, so keep an eye out for those!

On a final note, the Most Improved Cuber Award will be handed out next week at X-Awards, based on February time trials and the results from yesterday. The winner will receive a prize pack courtesy of the OLMC Rubik's Cube Club.

There will likely be one last send-off meeting for cubing club, stay tuned for when that will take place. Until then, happy cubing to all!

May 12th, 2012 by Michael Rose
Carmel Cube Day

The first ever Mount Carmel Cube Day will take place on Wednesday, May 23rd, 2012, during periods 2, 3, and 4 in the foyer of Our Lady of Mount Carmel Secondary School. There will be seven different events, music, snacks, merchandise for sale, and of course, lots and lots of cubing. Cubes can be preordered on the merchandise page. Any cuber from the school is welcome to participate. Please visit the registration page to sign up for the competition, or see Michael Rose in person. The last day for registration is Wednesday, May 16th, 2012. All those registered will be signed out of the periods listed above. We look forward to a fun day to promote cubing across OLMC, can't wait to see you all there!

April 29th, 2012 by Michael Rose
Results: OLMC Rubik's Cube Club Time Trials, April 2012

The OLMC Rubik's Cube Club Time Trials, April 2012 took place on April 25th. We had a good turnout once again, and some pictures were taken which will soon be posted on the site. A special thanks to Shawn Jozsa for providing extra snacks, despite the fact that he hoarded the dill pickle chips. A special thanks to Bryan Melchior for deciding to use Oreos in a less than conventional manner, to the amusement of the rest gathered. Finally, thank you to Ashley Moniz and Catherine Young for helping to take pictures.

We were lucky to have special guest Ashley Antony compete in the time trials. Thanks to him for his enthusiastic participation, and congratulations for his respectable showing, just missing out on third position.

Full results are now posted. Congratulations to the top three competitors in the 3x3 event. Their averages were as follows: Michael Rose, 16.72 seconds; Stephen Scicluna, 30.47; and Nicholas Murray, 57.34. Thank you to everyone who participated, judged, and scrambled.

Plans are in the works to hold the Mount Carmel Open, 2012, in early- to mid-June. This full-day event will consist of many events, lots of snacks, and as many competitors as we can muster. Hopefully we will get permission to hold it in the foyer, so we'll have a good number of spectators as well. Stay tuned for more info!
