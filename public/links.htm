Andy Klise's Rubik's Cube Guides

  • http://www.kungfoomanchu.com/

An amazing collection of well-organized, comprehensive, printable algorithm sheets. If you need a reference for learning any set of algorithms for almost all the main puzzles, this is your source. The most important for OLMC cubers is probably the 3x3x3 Speedcubing Guide. I am a big fan of the 2x2x2 CLL sheet, but I would stay away from the Beginner's Guide - unfortunately, I don't really think the algs provided are appropriate.

badmephisto

  • http://www.badmephisto.com/
  • http://www.youtube.com/user/badmephisto?ob=0

badmephisto's Youtube Channel has hands down the BEST video tutorials, you can't go wrong watching any of them. His website is a good resource for algorithms, his compilations are where I learned most of my F2L, PLL, and OLL algs. I would refer to Andy Klise's badmephisto page if you are interested in those. Otherwise, the website contains lots of useful and fun information, and probably the best method for progressing to ~15s solves. Definitely check it out!

canadianCUBING

  • http://www.canadiancubing.com/

The official webpage of the official cubing organization of Canada. It's the best place for updates in regards to all the Toronto area competitions. Keep an eye on the events page for details about upcoming comps.

Cubefreak

  • http://www.cubefreak.net/

An excellent source for a wide variety of cube related topics created by Shotaro Makisumi. There is an incredible amount of interesting pages here, and I have spent hours going through, finding something new every time. His page entitled Things cubers want non-cubers to understand is especially genius.

Lightake

  • http://lightake.com/products.do/category.Wholesale
    _Magic_Cube-13

One of the best online stores, they offer free shipping of hundreds of items. However, it does take a very long time (the packages come from China) and paying for faster shipping is expensive for small orders. Nonetheless, it is worth the wait, as the items are high quality and well packaged.

World Cube Association

  • http://worldcubeassociation.org/index.php

The go to site for all news in the cubing world. It contains your WCA page if you have competed in a competition before, as well as all the offical times, world records, and rankings of every competition around the world. The statistics page has some very interesting information, make sure to check it out!
